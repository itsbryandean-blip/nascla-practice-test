NEC 2026 — Don't Look These Up

Dwelling lighting: 2 VA/ft² (NOT 3 — exam trap)
Small appliance circuit: 1,500 VA each
Laundry circuit: 1,500 VA
Range demand (1 range ≤12 kW): 8 kW
EGC sizes: 15A/20A→14 AWG | 30A/60A→10 AWG | 100A→8 AWG | 200A→4 AWG
Small conductor max OCPD: 14ga→15A | 12ga→20A | 10ga→30A
Conduit fill: 1 wire=53% | 2 wires=31% | 3+ wires=40%
Box fill: #14=2.0 | #12=2.25 | #10=2.5 | #8=3.0 in³
Motor conductor: 125% × FLC
Motor CB (inv-time): 250% × FLC max
Motor dual-element fuse: 175% × FLC max
Motor overload: SF≥1.15 → 125% | SF<1.15 → 115%
Ground rod: 8 ft min length | 2 rods: 6 ft apart
Derating: 4–6 cond=80% | 7–9=65% | 10–20=50%
Demand factors: first 3,000VA@100% | next 117k@35% | rest@25%

OSHA 1926

Fall protection trigger: 6 ft (construction)
Guardrail top rail: 42 in ±3 in
Mid-rail: 21 in
Ladder extends above landing: 3 ft
Ladder angle: 1:4 (1 ft out per 4 ft up)
Trench shoring required at: 5 ft deep
Overhead power line clearance: 10 ft (up to 50kV)

NFPA 70E

Arc flash label must include: voltage, arc flash boundary, incident energy or PPE category, assessment date
Class A GFCI trips at: 5 mA
Energized work requires: written permit when deenergizing creates greater hazard or is infeasible

PMBOK 7 — EVM Formulas

EV = % complete × BAC
SV = EV − PV (positive = ahead)
CV = EV − AC (positive = under budget)
SPI = EV / PV (>1 = ahead)
CPI = EV / AC (>1 = under budget)
EAC = BAC / CPI

ASHRAE 90.1 — LPD Values

Office: 0.82 W/ft²
Warehouse: 0.33 W/ft²
Retail: 1.01 W/ft²
Storage: 0.50 W/ft²

Mike Holt Theory Formulas

Ohm's Law: E=IR | I=E/R | R=E/I
Power: P=IE | P=I²R | P=E²/R
Voltage drop: VD=IR | CM=K×I×D/VD
K (copper): 12.9 | K (aluminum): 21.2
3-phase kVA: 1.732 × V × I
√3 = 1.732
